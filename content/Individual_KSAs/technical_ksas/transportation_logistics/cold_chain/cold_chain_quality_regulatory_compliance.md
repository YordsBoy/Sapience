  - "Cold Chain"

description: Maintaining temperature‑controlled logistics for food and pharmaceuticals, applying Good Distribution Practice (GDP), Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point (HACCP), and International Air Transport Association (IATA) CEIV Pharma standards.
source_frameworks:
  - "ISO 23412:2020 Passenger Car Refrigerated Compartments and Insulated Boxes for Delivery Vehicles — Classification and Testing (ISO proprietary standard; conceptual alignment only)"
  - "WHO Good Distribution Practices for pharmaceutical products (World Health Organization — intergovernmental organization; publicly available)"
  - "EU Good Distribution Practice Guidelines 2022 (Guidelines of 5 November 2013 on Good Distribution Practice of medicinal products for human use, as updated) (European Union regulatory guidance; publicly available)"
  - "IATA CEIV Pharma certification program (International Air Transport Association — commercial trade association; conceptual alignment only)"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Reads temperature profiles; identifies cold‑chain breach indicators; uses data loggers.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Calibrates logger; packs products per lane qualification; validates insulated containers; records deviation events.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Validates lane; conducts route‑risk assessments; calibrates sensors; performs corrective‑action reports; aligns SOPs.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Designs lane qualification protocols; implements real‑time monitoring; runs CAPA; manages regulatory audits.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads global cold‑chain networks; shapes GDP guidelines; publishes cold‑chain reliability research.
