ksa_id: public_health_surveillance_epidemiology
label: Public Health Surveillance & Epidemiology
category: Technical
sector: healthcare_social_assistance
horizon: core
cluster_tags:
  - "Surveillance"
  - "Epidemiology"
description: >
  Designs and operates surveillance systems, performs epidemiologic investigations, and interprets data to guide public-health interventions and policy; analyses trends and communicates findings to stakeholders.
source_frameworks:
  - "CDC Updated Guidelines for Evaluating Public Health Surveillance Systems (U.S. government publication — Centers for Disease Control and Prevention; public domain)"
  - "CDC Public Health Information Network (PHIN) Messaging System specifications (U.S. government program — CDC; public domain)"
  - "WHO International Classification of Diseases 11th Revision (ICD-11) (intergovernmental organization; publicly available)"
  - "Council of State and Territorial Epidemiologists (CSTE) Applied Epidemiology Competencies (professional membership association; publicly available)"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Follows case-definition guidelines and enters surveillance data accurately.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Enters data into surveillance platforms; calculates incidence/prevalence; creates epi-curves; drafts investigation summaries.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Conducts field investigations; applies statistical tests; calculates R₀ and attack rates; communicates findings to stakeholders.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Leads multi-jurisdictional outbreak response; automates ELR, eCR, and ESSENCE feeds; performs modeling evaluates surveillance systems; publishes reports.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Designs national surveillance frameworks; integrates novel data streams (e.g., wastewater, syndromic); mentors epidemiologists; advises WHO/CDC.
