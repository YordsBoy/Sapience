Informal & Gray Economy
=======================

Roughly **60 % of the world’s workers** earn their livelihoods in informal or “gray” activities, with limited access to social protection, finance, or safety nets. ILO Recommendation 204 provides a rights-based pathway to formalization, while OECD’s *Non-Observed Economy* handbook improves measurement for better policy design. GSMA toolkits enable mobile-money and digital-financial literacy, critical for cash-based businesses. UN Women’s Safe-Markets programme spotlights vendor safety and gender equity, and FAO guidelines raise street-food hygiene standards. World Bank research links micro-skills and bootstrapping to poverty reduction in Sub-Saharan Africa, and IFAC plus USAID materials lower entry barriers to formal registration and accounting. These KSAs integrate such global guidance, equipping informal actors to thrive, de-risk, and transition when ready.
