  - "Orbital Debris"

description: >
  Designing and implementing measures—passivation, controlled re-entry, active debris removal—to minimise orbital debris in line with ISO 24113, Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC) FCC 5-year
  post-mission disposal guidelines; analyzes collision risk and implements passivation, de-orbit, or graveyard-orbit maneuvers.
source_frameworks:
  - "ISO 24113:2023 Space Systems — Space Debris Mitigation Requirements — ISO proprietary standard; conceptual alignment only"
  - "FCC Orbital Debris mitigation FAQ and rules (2024) — U.S. government — FCC; publicly available"
  - "IADC Space Debris Mitigation Guidelines (Rev. 2) — intergovernmental forum — Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC); publicly available"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Explains Kessler Syndrome; lists mitigation options; checks post-mission disposal (PMD) timelines; calculates 25-year LEO re-entry rule; identifies breakup causes.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Calculates delta-V for de-orbit burns; designs passivation procedures; runs DRAMA/MASTER risk models; sizes de-orbit propellant; files debris-mitigation plans.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Performs conjunction assessments; simulates breakup models; designs drag-sail or propulsion EOL kits; files FCC debris plan; coordinates disposal maneuvers.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Implements active-debris-removal concepts; deploys drag-sail or propulsion kits; analyses long-term orbital evolution; optimizes constellation shell disposals; validates compliance via ESA MASTER.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads international debris-mitigation working groups; innovates ADR (active debris removal) tech; shapes ISO/IADC updates; publishes debris-risk analyses.
