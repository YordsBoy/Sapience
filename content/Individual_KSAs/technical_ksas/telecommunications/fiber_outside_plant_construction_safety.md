description: Managing fiber‑optic outside‑plant (OSP) construction—trenching, aerial installation, splicing—while adhering to Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) standards, National Electrical Safety Code (NESC), and industry best practices for excavation and working at height.
source_frameworks: ["OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Construction","NESC 2023","Fiber Broadband Association OSP Handbook"]
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Conducts job‑site briefings; dons Personal Protective Equipment (PPE); identifies underground‑utility marks.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Implements lockout/tagout on aerial networks; uses fall‑arrest gear; maintains splice enclosures.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Coordinates one‑call dig permits; analyses tension and sag calculations; ensures traffic‑control plans.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Designs micro‑trench routes; supervises multi‑contractor builds; audits safety compliance KPIs.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads nationwide fiber rollouts; shapes OSP safety guidelines; publishes construction‑safety studies.
