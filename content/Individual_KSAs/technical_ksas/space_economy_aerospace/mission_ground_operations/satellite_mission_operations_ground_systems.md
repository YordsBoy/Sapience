description: >
  Planning and executing satellite mission operations—telemetry, tracking, and command (TT&C); flight-dynamic analyses; anomaly response—using NASA Mission Operations Directorate (MOD) and Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems (CCSDS) standards.
source_frameworks:
  - "NASA Conjunction Assessment Risk Analysis (CARA) process documentation — U.S. government — NASA; publicly available"
  - "NASA Academy of Program/Project and Engineering Leadership (APPEL) mission operations curriculum — U.S. government — NASA; publicly available"
  - "CCSDS 232.1-B-2 TM Synchronization and Channel Coding — intergovernmental consortium — CCSDS (NASA/ESA/JAXA/et al.); publicly available"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Reads basic telemetry packets; monitors ground-station schedules; follows operations checklists.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Executes command loads; performs state-of-health trending; performs routine pass scripts; files TLE updates; logs anomalies.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Conducts orbit determination; coordinates station-keeping burns; implements contingency procedures.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Optimises ground-network utilization; leads mission-readiness reviews (MRRs); integrates automation scripts and ground-station as-a-service.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Oversees multi-satellite constellations; defines anomaly-response playbooks; develops mission-ops standards; publishes operations-efficiency research.
