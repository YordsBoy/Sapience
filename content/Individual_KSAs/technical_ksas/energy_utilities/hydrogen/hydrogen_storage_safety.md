category: Technical

horizon: emerging
cluster_tags:
  - "Hydrogen"
  - "Process Safety"
description: >
  Operates and maintains high-pressure compressed and cryogenic liquid hydrogen
  storage systems in compliance with NFPA 2 and ISO 19880-1 safety requirements,
  including leak detection, pressure-relief management, dispensing controls, and
  emergency response. See also `hydrogen_production_operations` for electrolyser
  and reformer operations upstream of storage.
source_frameworks:
  - "ISO 19880-1:2020 Gaseous Hydrogen — Fuelling Stations — General Requirements (ISO proprietary standard; conceptual alignment only — CaliberPath does not assert reproduction rights)"
  - "NFPA 2 Hydrogen Technologies Code (proprietary standard — National Fire Protection Association; conceptual alignment only — CaliberPath does not assert reproduction rights. Note: NFPA 2 is a copyrighted private-organization standard; it is not public domain despite regulatory adoption.)"
  - "OSHA 29 CFR 1910.103 — Hydrogen (U.S. government regulation — Occupational Safety and Health Administration; public domain)"
  - "U.S. DOE Hydrogen Safety Best Practices resources (U.S. government publication — U.S. Department of Energy; public domain)"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Describes the key hazard properties of hydrogen (flammability range 4–75% v/v, low ignition energy, buoyancy, invisible flame); identifies the major storage vessel types (Type I–IV composite cylinders, cryogenic dewars) and distinguishes compressed-gas from liquid hydrogen storage configurations.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Inspects high-pressure storage vessels, piping, and fittings per scheduled maintenance procedures; verifies pressure-relief device settings and documentation; executes lock-out/tag-out on hydrogen systems; responds to fixed-point gas detector alarms per site emergency response plan.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Conducts leak surveys using portable electrochemical and catalytic gas detectors; manages dispensing operations including pre-cooling checks and break-away coupling integrity; maintains storage inventory records and calculates quantity-on-hand; performs and documents functional tests of pressure-relief and excess-flow valves.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Designs storage area layouts in compliance with NFPA 2 setback, ventilation, and ignition-source exclusion requirements; leads facility Hazard and Operability (HAZOP) studies and quantitative risk assessments for hydrogen storage systems; develops site emergency response plans and drills; evaluates material compatibility for hydrogen service (hydrogen embrittlement screening per ASME standards).
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads enterprise hydrogen safety programs across multi-site operations; contributes to standards development (ISO TC 197, NFPA 2 revision cycles); mentors safety engineers and first responders; advises on emerging storage modalities including solid-state metal-hydride, cryo-compressed, and underground cavern storage.
