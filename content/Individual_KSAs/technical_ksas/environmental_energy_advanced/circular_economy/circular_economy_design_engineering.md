description: >
  Applies cradle-to-cradle principles to product and infrastructure design; prioritises reuse, remanufacturing, recycling, and regenerative materials.
source_frameworks:
  - "Ellen MacArthur Foundation Circular Economy principles and Circular Design Guide (nonprofit organization — Ellen MacArthur Foundation; conceptual alignment only)"
  - "ISO 59010:2023 Circular Economy — Guidance on the Transition of Business Models and Related Activities (ISO proprietary standard; conceptual alignment only — CaliberPath does not assert reproduction rights)"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Defines linear vs circular models; identifies waste streams; calculates recyclability; recognizes take-back schemes.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Applies design-for-disassembly principles; selects recyclable materials; maps and analyses end-of-life pathways.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Develops product-as-service models; quantifies circularity indicators; conducts reverse-logistics studies.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Integrates circularity into product development stage-gates; measures economic returns; secures certification (Cradle to Cradle).
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads systemic CE transitions at city or sector scale and/or multi-product circular portfolios; shapes circular-economy standards; publishes design-engineering research.
