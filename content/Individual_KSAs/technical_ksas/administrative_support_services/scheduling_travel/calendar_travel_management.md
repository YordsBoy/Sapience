description: >
  Plans and coordinates complex domestic and international travel itineraries for executives and teams — including flights, accommodation, ground transport, and visa requirements — while managing the associated calendar implications across time zones; applies corporate travel policy, preferred-vendor programs, and duty-of-care obligations to optimise cost, compliance, and traveller experience.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Identifies the key components of a business travel itinerary (flights, hotel, ground transport, meeting schedule); explains how time-zone differences affect calendar coordination and traveller fatigue.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Books domestic flights and hotels through approved portals applying corporate rate and vendor preferences; blocks travel time on the traveller's calendar; collects required documentation (boarding passes, hotel confirmation, ground transport) and distributes a consolidated itinerary.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Coordinates multi-city and multi-traveller international itineraries including visa and passport requirements, ESTA/eTA applications, and travel insurance; manages calendar implications across multiple time zones; updates bookings in response to flight changes and communicates proactively with travellers.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Negotiates preferred corporate rates with travel management companies and hotel programmes; analyses travel spend reports to identify policy compliance gaps; manages executive calendars during complex travel periods, coordinating with counterparts in other offices and time zones to protect critical commitments.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Designs and maintains the organisation's travel programme including policy, preferred-vendor framework, and duty-of-care protocols; integrates travel management systems with expense-reporting and calendar platforms; mentors travel coordinators and trains executives on self-serve booking tools; drives continuous improvement in cost, compliance, and traveller wellbeing metrics.
