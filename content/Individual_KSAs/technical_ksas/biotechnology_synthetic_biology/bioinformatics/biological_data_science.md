---
ksa_id: biological_data_science
label: Biological Data Science & Bioinformatics
category: Technical
sector: biotechnology_synthetic_biology
horizon: core
cluster_tags:
  - "Bioinformatics"
  - "Data Analysis"
description: >
  Analyses multi-omics datasets (genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic) using computational pipelines; applies statistics and machine-learning to uncover biological insight and guide
  experimental design.
source_frameworks:
  - "NIH Bioinformatics Core Competencies — U.S. government — NIH; public domain"
  - "ELIXIR Bioinformatics Core Competencies — European research infrastructure consortium; publicly available"
  - "FAIR Data Principles (Wilkinson et al. 2016) — open community standard; publicly available"
  - "Global Alliance for Genomics and Health (GA4GH) Standards — international standards consortium; publicly available"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Describes the categories of biological data generated by modern sequencing and proteomics platforms (genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic); explains how bioinformatics pipelines convert raw data into biological insights and why data quality affects downstream decisions.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Interprets standard bioinformatics outputs including differential expression tables, variant reports, and pathway enrichment summaries in the context of project objectives; identifies key data quality indicators from QC summary reports provided by analytical teams.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Reviews and interrogates bioinformatics analysis reports; evaluates statistical significance and biological effect sizes in multi-omics findings; applies FAIR data principles to data management decisions; specifies analysis requirements when commissioning bioinformatics work from internal or external teams.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Defines analytical workflow requirements and acceptance criteria for bioinformatics projects; evaluates pipeline output validity and integrates multi-omics insights into regulatory strategy or research decision-making; leads data governance implementation in compliance with FAIR standards and GA4GH frameworks.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads the biological data science strategy for a research or product development organisation; designs analytical governance frameworks and data-sharing architectures; influences consortium and industry standards; mentors project leaders on evidence-based integration of computational biology into strategic decisions.
---
