description: Conducting property valuations using sales‑comparison, cost, and income approaches per Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) and Appraisal Institute (AI) MAI designation requirements.
source_frameworks:
  - "RICS Red Book Global"
  - "USPAP 2024‑25 Edition"
  - "Appraisal Institute MAI Curriculum"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Lists three approaches; defines highest & best use (HBU); collects comparable sales; measures gross living area (GLA); confirms zoning.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Collects comps; calculates cost new and depreciation; prepares income‑capitalisation summaries; applies cap-rate; writes restricted appraisal reports.
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Performs discounted‑cash‑flow (DCF) analysis; reconciles multiple valuation approaches; adjusts for market conditions; completes appraisal report.
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Addresses special assumptions; conducts highest‑and‑best‑use studies; defends appraisals in litigation; complies with USPAP record‑keeping.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Chairs valuation committee; leads complex portfolio valuations; mentors trainee appraisers; contributes to valuation standards.
